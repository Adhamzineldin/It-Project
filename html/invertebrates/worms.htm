Earthworms

Earthworms are perhaps the most well-known type of worm. They belong to the class Oligochaeta within the phylum Annelida. Earthworms are found in soil and play a crucial role in aerating and fertilizing the soil. They feed on organic matter, breaking it down into nutrients that plants can absorb.

Earthworms are typically beneficial to gardens and agriculture, as their activities improve soil structure and fertility.

Flatworms

Flatworms are a diverse group of worms belonging to the phylum Platyhelminthes. They have flattened, ribbon-like bodies and are found in both terrestrial and aquatic environments. Some flatworms are free-living, while others are parasitic, infecting animals and humans.

Flatworms exhibit a wide range of feeding habits, including scavenging, predation, and parasitism. Some common examples of flatworms include planarians, tapeworms, and flukes.

Roundworms (Nematoda)

Roundworms, also known as nematodes, are cylindrical worms belonging to the phylum Nematoda. They are found in diverse habitats, including soil, water, and the bodies of plants and animals. Roundworms exhibit a wide range of feeding habits, including parasitism, predation, and scavenging.

Some nematodes are beneficial, contributing to nutrient cycling and pest control, while others are harmful, causing diseases in plants, animals, and humans. Common examples of nematodes include hookworms, pinworms, and root-knot nematodes.
