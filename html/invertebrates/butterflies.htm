Monarch Butterfly

The monarch butterfly (Danaus plexippus) is known for its distinctive orange and black wings and is famous for its annual migration. It is one of the most recognizable and well-studied butterflies in the world. Monarchs are found primarily in North America, but they also inhabit other regions such as Central and South America, as well as Australia and several Pacific islands.

These butterflies undergo a long migration journey, with some individuals traveling thousands of miles from breeding grounds in the United States and Canada to overwintering sites in Mexico. Monarchs are also known for their fascinating life cycle, which includes four stages: egg, larva (caterpillar), pupa (chrysalis), and adult butterfly.

Swallowtail Butterfly

The swallowtail butterfly is a large and colorful butterfly characterized by its distinctive swallow-like tails extending from the hindwings. There are over 550 species of swallowtails found across the world, with varying colors, patterns, and sizes.

These butterflies are known for their graceful flight and are often found in gardens, meadows, and wooded areas. Swallowtails typically feed on nectar from flowers, and their caterpillars are known for their unique defense mechanisms, such as emitting strong-smelling chemicals to deter predators.

Peacock Butterfly

The peacock butterfly (Aglais io) is a colorful butterfly found in Europe and temperate Asia. It is named for the distinctive eye-like markings on its wings, which resemble those of a peacock's tail feathers.

Peacock butterflies are commonly found in meadows, parks, and gardens, where they feed on the nectar of various flowers. They are known for their striking appearance and are often admired by butterfly enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.
